Scotland is a country in Europe and is part of the island of Great Britain (Europe's Largest Island) alongside England, Wales, and Northern Island. This lush beautiful country is bursting with green spaces, lush forests, towering mountains and vast lochs (the Scottish word for lakes!).

This country can be roughly divided into three areas - Lowlands, Highlands and Islands. The Lowlands are known for their fertile farmland and thick woodlands, the Highlands for their towering mountains, sweeping moorland and deep lochs, and the Island for their compact wild landscapes, beautiful beaches and far-reaching sea views.

Places to Visit in Scotland

Edinburgh Castle

Perched atop Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle is a historic fortress that offers panoramic views of the city and houses Scotland's Crown Jewels. It has played a key role in Scottish history for centuries.

Kelvingrove Art Museum

Located in Glasgow, Kelvingrove Art Museum is home to an extensive collection of art, artifacts, and natural history exhibits. Its impressive architecture and free admission make it a popular cultural destination.

Culzean Castle

Overlooking the Firth of Clyde, Culzean Castle is an elegant 18th-century mansion known for its grand interiors and scenic cliffside gardens. The castle is a popular spot for exploring Scottish history and nature.

Portree, Isle of Skye

Portree is a picturesque harbor town on the Isle of Skye, famous for its colorful buildings and dramatic coastal landscapes. It's a gateway for exploring the island's natural beauty and hiking trails.

The Calanais Standing Stones

The Calanais Standing Stones are an ancient stone circle located on the Isle of Lewis, dating back over 5,000 years. These mysterious megaliths are steeped in legend and offer insight into prehistoric life.

The Jacobite Steam Train

The Jacobite Steam Train is a historic train that travels through the scenic West Highland Line, famously passing over the Glenfinnan Viaduct. It is often associated with its appearance in the Harry Potter films.

Facts about Scotland

The official animal of Scotland is the Unicorn.

Scotland has approximately 790 Islands.

Scotland is home to the oldest tree in Europe. It is a twisted yew, and it has been around for 3,000 years.

There are over 600 square miles of freshwater lakes.
